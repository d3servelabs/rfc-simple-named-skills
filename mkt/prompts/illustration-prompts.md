# Illustration Prompts for DVS Long Article

Style: Clean white background, dark navy and teal foreground, light gray grid. 1.91:1 wide aspect ratio (OpenGraph). Minimal, technical but approachable.

## 1. "The Octopus" (for: AI Agents Are Powerful)

Analogy: An agent is like an octopus — one brain, many arms, each reaching into a different system simultaneously.

> Clean technical illustration, 1.91:1 wide aspect ratio, white background. A friendly but imposing robotic octopus in dark navy ink, centered. Each of its eight tentacles reaches into a different real-world object: a server rack, a filing cabinet drawer, an envelope being sealed, a code terminal screen, a cloud, a credit card payment terminal, a padlock being unlocked, a phone. The octopus looks calm and competent. Subtle light gray grid in background. No text. Flat vector style with teal and navy color palette, touches of orange on the objects being touched. Clean, editorial illustration style.

## 2. "Wolf in Sheep's Clothing" (for: We Need Trust More Than Ever)

Editorial illustration, no text.

## 3. "The Street Address" (for: Skills Are New. Verifiability Is Not.)

Analogy: A domain is like a street address on a building — you know who lives there because you can see the building, read the address, and verify it on a map. No new ID system needed.

> Clean technical illustration, 1.91:1 wide aspect ratio, white background. A row of three storefronts on a street, drawn in clean architectural line art. Each building has a clearly visible street address plate above its door: "123 Microsoft Ave", "456 Acme Blvd", "789 GitHub Lane". Inside each storefront window, you can see skill document icons on display shelves. A friendly robot walks down the sidewalk reading the address plates confidently. Street signs and building numbers are crisp and legible. Overhead, a subtle dotted line connects from a globe icon (DNS) to the street signs, and a small lock icon (TLS) sits on each door. Flat vector style, dark navy and teal, warm orange accent on the address plates. No other text. Clean editorial illustration.

## 4. "The Fenced Yard" (for: What DVS Actually Specifies — Trust Root)

Analogy: A Trust Root is like a property fence — everything inside the fence belongs to the owner. On a shared platform (apartment building), your trust boundary is your unit, not the whole building.

> Clean technical illustration, 1.91:1 wide aspect ratio, white background. Left side: a standalone house with a neat fence around its yard, a mailbox labeled "acme.com" at the gate. Inside the fenced yard, several document icons sit neatly on the lawn. A green checkmark floats above the fence. Right side: a tall apartment building labeled "github.com" at the entrance. The whole building is outlined in light gray (not trusted as a whole). One apartment unit on the third floor is highlighted with a teal border and labeled "acme-org", with document icons visible through its window and a green checkmark. Other apartment windows are dimmed. A red X near the building entrance indicates the whole building is not one trust boundary. Flat vector style, dark navy and teal, light gray grid background. No other text. Clean editorial illustration.

## 5. "The Parcel — Unknown Sender vs. Return Address" (for: hero/summary image)

Analogy: Today's skills are like parcels with no return address — you don't know who sent them. DVS is like requiring a verified return address on every package.

> Clean technical illustration, 1.91:1 wide aspect ratio, white background, split composition with a thin vertical dashed line. Left side: a robot at a desk looking confused, surrounded by brown parcels/packages with question marks where the return address should be. Some parcels are slightly torn or suspicious-looking. The desk is messy. Muted gray and navy tones. Right side: the same robot at the same desk, now confident, receiving parcels that each have a clear, bold return address label with a green verified checkmark stamp on it. The desk is tidy. Bright teal and navy tones. Flat vector style, dark navy and teal palette. No text except the dashed question marks and checkmarks. Clean editorial illustration.
